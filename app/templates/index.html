How It Works

Smart analysis,
every rep.

  1. 01
    Pose Detection

    MediaPipe BlazePose tracks 33 body landmarks in real time from your webcam feed — no wearables, no markers, no setup.

  2. 02
    Form Analysis

    Joint angles, body ratios and symmetry metrics are computed every frame, then compared against biomechanical thresholds from peer-reviewed literature.

  3. 03
    Dedicated Detector

    Each exercise has its own state machine and form thresholds. Squat doesn't share logic with deadlift. Specialised, not general.

  4. 04
    Live Feedback

    Colour-coded skeleton overlay, on-screen cues and a continuous form score tell you exactly what to fix, rep by rep, set by set.

        About the Project

        Smarter training,
        better results.

        A real-time computer vision system for exercise form evaluation. Built as a BSc Computer Science final year project at the University of Greenwich.

        Form is the line between progress and injury. FORMA puts that line on screen, in real time.
        The Name

        FORMA.

        FORMA is Italian and Latin for form. The whole project hinges on one idea — that how you move matters more than how much you move. The wordmark is set in Bebas Neue, condensed and architectural, with a thin gold underline that runs the full width of the letters. The tagline is the rule the app trains you to follow.

        Train with form.

        The Problem

        Poor Form Leads to Injuries

        Incorrect exercise form is the leading cause of gym injuries. Personal trainers are expensive and not always available. Most people train alone without feedback on their technique, risking long-term damage to joints and muscles that could easily be prevented with real-time guidance.

        The Solution

        AI That Watches Your Form

        FORMA uses computer vision and biomechanics to read your body in real time, giving you the same feedback a personal trainer would — instantly and for free. Open your webcam, choose an exercise, and train with form.

        Preview Feature 02 — Coach Chatbot

        Ask your coach,
        any time.

        A chatbot that knows every session you've logged. Ask about your trends, your weak lifts, or what to train tomorrow. Answers come from your real history.

        How many push-ups have I done in the last 30 days?
        You've completed 847 push-ups across 12 sessions in the last 30 days. Your average form score climbed from 0.71 to 0.83 — a clear upward trend.
        What's my weakest exercise?
        Your deadlift averages 0.62, well below your other lifts. The recurring issue is back rounding past hip-flexion of 80°. Want me to build a 2-week mobility plan focused on hamstrings and bracing?
        Yes please.
        On it. Plan saved as Deadlift Reset · 14 days. I'll show progress on your dashboard each morning.
        Try asking
        • "Am I improving week over week?"
        • "When did my squat form peak?"
        • "What should I train tomorrow?"
        • "Show me my longest streak."
        • "Build me a 1,000 push-up plan."
        Powered by

        A custom RAG pipeline with prompt caching. Reads only the slice of your history that's relevant to each question — never sends raw video or images.

        Preview Feature 03 — Plans & Goals

        A plan that
        knows you.

        Tell your coach a goal — "1,000 push-ups in 10 days", "fix my deadlift form", "20-minute morning routine" — and it builds a day-by-day plan from your actual fitness baseline.

        Strength

        The Foundation

        Three sessions a week, six weeks. Squat. Press. Pull.

        • Mon — Squat day, 5×5
        • Wed — Press day, 5×5
        • Fri — Deadlift day, 1×5
        • Two days of mobility
        • Tracked: form score, depth, lockout
        Hypertrophy

        Volume Block

        Push-pull-legs, four sessions, eight weeks. Chase the pump.

        • Higher rep ranges, 8-12
        • Bench, overhead, curls, dips
        • Form score gates rest periods
        • Auto-deload week 4
        • Tracked: total reps, fatigue curves
        Mobility

        The Reset

        Twenty minutes a morning. No equipment. Just movement quality.

        • Daily, 14 days
        • Plank, lunge, bodyweight squat
        • Form score over rep count
        • Coach prompts on hip sag
        • Tracked: hold time, alignment
