## 7.1  Guidelines and standards

### haff2016nsca
**Harvard.** Haff, G.G. and Triplett, N.T. (eds.) (2016) *Essentials of Strength Training and Conditioning*. 4th edn. Champaign, IL: Human Kinetics.
**ISBN.** 9781492501626.
**Annotation.** The NSCA's official textbook, canonical reference for resistance-training programme design, exercise technique, and progression principles. Cite as the authoritative source behind FORMA's exercise-technique cues and plan-progression logic.
**Suggested quote.** "Proper exercise technique is the foundation of a safe and effective resistance training programme."
**Pages.** Ch. 13 "Exercise Technique for Free Weight and Machine Training" (pp. 351-408); Ch. 17 "Program Design for Resistance Training".

### acsm2021guidelines
**Harvard.** American College of Sports Medicine (2021) *ACSM's Guidelines for Exercise Testing and Prescription*. 11th edn. Edited by G. Liguori. Philadelphia, PA: Wolters Kluwer.
**ISBN.** 9781975150181.
**Annotation.** ACSM flagship reference used internationally to set FITT-VP parameters. Cite when justifying FORMA's plan-engine dosing and when framing exercise-screening/disclaimer logic.

### garber2011acsm — ACSM position stand
**Harvard.** Garber, C.E. *et al.* (2011) 'American College of Sports Medicine Position Stand: Quantity and quality of exercise', *Med. Sci. Sports Exerc.*, 43(7), pp. 1334-1359.
**DOI.** 10.1249/MSS.0b013e318213fefb.
**Annotation.** ACSM position stand recommending 2-3 resistance-training sessions per week involving major muscle groups. Underpins FORMA's default weekly frequency.
**Suggested quote.** "Each adult should perform resistance exercises for each of the major muscle groups 2-3 days per week."

### bull2020who — WHO 2020 PA guidelines
**Harvard.** Bull, F.C. *et al.* (2020) 'World Health Organization 2020 guidelines on physical activity and sedentary behaviour', *British Journal of Sports Medicine*, 54(24), pp. 1451-1462.
**DOI.** 10.1136/bjsports-2020-102955.
**Annotation.** Current WHO guidelines — 150-300 min moderate aerobic per week plus muscle-strengthening on 2+ days. Justifies the public-health framing of FORMA's problem statement and the minimum dosage plans try to meet.

## 7.2  Injury epidemiology in resistance training

### keogh2017epi
**Harvard.** Keogh, J.W.L. and Winwood, P.W. (2017) 'The epidemiology of injuries across the weight-training sports', *Sports Medicine*, 47(3), pp. 479-501.
**DOI.** 10.1007/s40279-016-0575-0.
**Annotation.** Systematic review across bodybuilding, powerlifting, weightlifting, strongman, and CrossFit. Cornerstone for FORMA's "resistance-training injury is technique-sensitive" motivation.
**Suggested quote.** "The shoulder, lower back, knee, elbow, and wrist/hand were generally the most commonly injured anatomical locations."

### aasa2017injuries
**Harvard.** Aasa, U. *et al.* (2017) 'Injuries among weightlifters and powerlifters: a systematic review', *Br. J. Sports Med.*, 51(4), pp. 211-219.
**DOI.** 10.1136/bjsports-2016-096037.
**Annotation.** Quantifies injury rate (~2-5 / 1000 training hours) and localisation in weightlifting and powerlifting. Supports the claim that FORMA's target lifts carry non-trivial, technique-modifiable injury risk.

### siewe2011powerlifting
**Harvard.** Siewe, J. *et al.* (2011) 'Injuries and overuse syndromes in powerlifting', *Int. J. Sports Med.*, 32(9), pp. 703-711.
**DOI.** 10.1055/s-0031-1277207.
**Annotation.** Questionnaire of 245 competitive powerlifters: 43.3% reported problems; shoulder/lower-back/knee most affected. Useful framing for squat + deadlift as technique-sensitive.

### bengtsson2018powerlifting — Per-lift injury attribution
**Harvard.** Bengtsson, V., Berglund, L. and Aasa, U. (2018) 'Narrative review of injuries in powerlifting with special reference to their association to the squat, bench press and deadlift', *BMJ Open Sport & Exercise Medicine*, 4(1), p. e000382.
**DOI.** 10.1136/bmjsem-2018-000382.
**Annotation.** Links specific injuries to specific lifts — lower-back/meniscal → deadlift; shoulder impingement/pec rupture → bench press; patellar tendinopathy → squat. Cite when tying FORMA's per-exercise injury rationale to epidemiology.

### kolber2014recreational — Recreational lifters + shoulders
**Harvard.** Kolber, M.J. *et al.* (2014) 'Characteristics of shoulder impingement in the recreational weight-training population', *J. Strength Cond. Res.*, 28(4), pp. 1081-1089.
**DOI.** 10.1519/JSC.0000000000000250.
**Annotation.** Implicates lateral raises and upright rows beyond 90° abduction in subacromial impingement. Directly motivates FORMA's lateral-raise form check.

### kolber2010shoulder
**Harvard.** Kolber, M.J. *et al.* (2010) 'Shoulder injuries attributed to resistance training: a brief review', *J. Strength Cond. Res.*, 24(6), pp. 1696-1704.
**DOI.** 10.1519/JSC.0b013e3181dc4330.
**Annotation.** Up to 36% of resistance-training injuries occur at the shoulder complex. Grounds FORMA's shoulder-centric form checks for lateral-raise and (retired) overhead movements.
**Suggested quote.** "Up to 36% of reported injuries among resistance training enthusiasts involve the shoulder complex."

### haupt2001upper
**Harvard.** Haupt, H.A. (2001) 'Upper extremity injuries associated with strength training', *Clin. Sports Med.*, 20(3), pp. 481-490.
**DOI.** 10.1016/S0278-5919(05)70264-7.
**Annotation.** Clinical review of upper-extremity injury patterns — pectoralis rupture, anterior shoulder instability from wide-grip bench. Background for why heavy upper-body pressing was excluded from the home-friendly set.

## 7.3  Assessment + measurement reliability

### kolber2011inclinometer
**Harvard.** Kolber, M.J. *et al.* (2011) 'The reliability and minimal detectable change of shoulder mobility measurements using a digital inclinometer', *Physiother. Theory Pract.*, 27(2), pp. 176-184.
**DOI.** 10.3109/09593985.2010.481011.
**Annotation.** Shows reliability of angle-based shoulder-ROM measurement (ICCs 0.83-0.94). Supports FORMA's use of joint angles from MediaPipe as a valid proxy for ROM.

## 7.4  Progressive overload + hypertrophy science

### kraemer2004fundamentals
**Harvard.** Kraemer, W.J. and Ratamess, N.A. (2004) 'Fundamentals of resistance training: progression and exercise prescription', *Med. Sci. Sports Exerc.*, 36(4), pp. 674-688.
**DOI.** 10.1249/01.MSS.0000121945.36635.61.
**Annotation.** Foundational paper on progressive overload. Justifies the structure of FORMA's plan engine (weekly progression, `target_weeks`, milestone-driven load increases).
**Suggested quote.** "Progression in resistance training is a dynamic process that requires exercise prescription."

### schoenfeld2017doseresponse
**Harvard.** Schoenfeld, B.J., Ogborn, D. and Krieger, J.W. (2017) 'Dose-response relationship between weekly resistance training volume and increases in muscle mass: a systematic review and meta-analysis', *J. Sports Sci.*, 35(11), pp. 1073-1082.
**DOI.** 10.1080/02640414.2016.1210197.
**Annotation.** Meta-analysis showing a positive dose-response between weekly sets and hypertrophy. Underpins FORMA's weekly-volume recommendations.

### schoenfeld2010hypertrophy
**Harvard.** Schoenfeld, B.J. (2010) 'The mechanisms of muscle hypertrophy and their application to resistance training', *J. Strength Cond. Res.*, 24(10), pp. 2857-2872.
**DOI.** 10.1519/JSC.0b013e3181e840f3.
**Annotation.** Mechanical tension, metabolic stress, muscle damage as the three hypertrophy drivers. Useful when explaining why FORMA insists on full ROM (tension argument).

### schoenfeld2019volume
**Harvard.** Schoenfeld, B.J. *et al.* (2019) 'Resistance training volume enhances muscle hypertrophy but not strength in trained men', *Med. Sci. Sports Exerc.*, 51(1), pp. 94-103.
**DOI.** 10.1249/MSS.0000000000001764.
**Annotation.** RCT: higher volume → better hypertrophy, equivalent strength. Cite when explaining FORMA's differentiated plan output by goal (strength vs muscle).

## 7.5  Adherence + supervised vs unsupervised training

### dishman1982adherence
**Harvard.** Dishman, R.K. (1982) 'Compliance/adherence in health-related exercise', *Health Psychology*, 1(3), pp. 237-267.
**DOI.** 10.1037/0278-6133.1.3.237.
**Annotation.** Seminal paper organising exercise-adherence evidence. Classic reference for "dropout from exercise programmes is ~50% within 6 months" — a key motivation for FORMA's engagement features.

### lacroix2017supervised
**Harvard.** Lacroix, A., Hortobagyi, T., Beurskens, R. and Granacher, U. (2017) 'Effects of supervised vs. unsupervised training programs on balance and muscle strength in older adults: a systematic review and meta-analysis', *Sports Medicine*, 47(11), pp. 2341-2361.
**DOI.** 10.1007/s40279-017-0747-6.
**Annotation.** Supervised training outperforms unsupervised home training on balance/strength in older adults. Frames the gap FORMA tries to close — supervision-equivalent feedback without a human trainer.
**Suggested quote.** "Supervised training programs appear to be more effective than unsupervised programs."

### thompson2020trends — Fitness-industry trends
**Harvard.** Thompson, W.R. (2019) 'Worldwide Survey of Fitness Trends for 2020', *ACSM's Health & Fitness Journal*, 23(6), pp. 10-18.
**DOI.** 10.1249/FIT.0000000000000526.
**Annotation.** ACSM's annual Delphi survey, with wearable tech / HIIT / home fitness prominent. Supports FORMA's timeliness claim.

## 7.6  Digital coaching efficacy + BCTs

### direito2014bct
**Harvard.** Direito, A. *et al.* (2014) 'Do physical activity and dietary smartphone applications incorporate evidence-based behaviour change techniques?', *BMC Public Health*, 14, p. 646.
**DOI.** 10.1186/1471-2458-14-646.
**Annotation.** Content analysis of PA/diet apps: most incorporate few evidence-based BCTs. Motivates FORMA's deliberate inclusion of goal-setting, self-monitoring, feedback, and reinforcement.

### schoeppe2016apps
**Harvard.** Schoeppe, S. *et al.* (2016) 'Efficacy of interventions that use apps to improve diet, physical activity and sedentary behaviour: a systematic review', *Int. J. Behav. Nutr. Phys. Act.*, 13, p. 127.
**DOI.** 10.1186/s12966-016-0454-y.
**Annotation.** Systematic review (27 studies) showing apps can produce significant PA/diet/sedentary-behaviour improvements. Evidence that the digital-coaching category FORMA sits in can meaningfully change behaviour.

### romeo2019smartphoneapps
**Harvard.** Romeo, A. *et al.* (2019) 'Can smartphone apps increase physical activity? Systematic review and meta-analysis', *Journal of Medical Internet Research*, 21(3), p. e12053.
**DOI.** 10.2196/12053.
**Annotation.** Meta-analysis of 9 RCTs: modest but significant increase in objective PA. Evidence of plausible behaviour-change effect.

## 7.7  CV-based form coaching (prior art to FORMA)

### andersonyoumove2013
**Harvard.** Anderson, F. *et al.* (2013) 'YouMove: enhancing movement training with an augmented reality mirror', in *Proceedings of ACM UIST 2013*, pp. 311-320.
**DOI.** 10.1145/2501988.2502045.
**Annotation.** Early and highly cited AR-mirror movement-training system — 2× short-term retention vs video demonstration. Foundational prior work in the CV movement-coach literature that FORMA extends.

### chenposetrainer2020
**Harvard.** Chen, S. and Yang, R.R. (2020) 'Pose Trainer: correcting exercise posture using pose estimation', *arXiv preprint* arXiv:2006.11718.
**Annotation.** Closest direct prior work to FORMA — OpenPose + ~100-video dataset + geometric-heuristic + ML evaluation across four exercises. Comparable baseline for "what FORMA improves on" (more exercises, real-time streaming, MediaPipe landmarks, richer feedback engine).
